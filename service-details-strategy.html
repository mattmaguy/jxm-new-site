FAQ

Common questions about how growth strategy works

Most engagements run six to ten weeks, depending on data quality and stakeholder availability. The first two weeks are diagnostic. The middle is modeling, channel strategy, and roadmap. The last stretch is alignment — getting leadership, finance, and the marketing team to all agree on the plan and the definition of success.

Strategy is project-based. Implementation can run on retainer if you want us in the trenches with your team. Most clients start with a strategy engagement, then decide whether to keep us on for execution. We don't push retainers. They only make sense when there's enough real complexity to actually need outside operators.

A diagnostic memo, a market and spend model you can edit, a channel strategy with weighted recommendations, a twelve-month roadmap with quarters and owners, and a measurement framework wired to your reporting. Plus a final session walking leadership through every recommendation so your team owns the plan, not just passively receives it.

Yes, often. Strategy doesn't replace your existing partners — it tells them what to chase. We brief your creative agency, your media partner, and your internal team on the same plan with the same numbers. The whole point is to get everyone you're already paying to actually pull in the same direction.

Then we pressure-test it. We model the assumptions, stress the channel mix, and tell you where it breaks. Sometimes the strategy is right and the execution is the problem. Sometimes it's the opposite. Either way, you walk out knowing what holds up under real numbers and what was a story nobody questioned.

We can tell you what's underperforming and why. People decisions are yours to make, not ours. But we'll show the work — the channel that's quietly losing money, the role that's structurally misaligned, the partner whose deliverables don't match the invoice. You'll have the math. The conversation that follows is fully yours.

Fixed fee tied to scope. We scope the engagement up front — audit, modeling, roadmap, working sessions — and quote a single number. No hourly billing, surprise invoices, or retainer creep. If scope changes, we tell you what it costs before we touch it. The number on the SOW is the number.
